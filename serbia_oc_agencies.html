Serbia — organised crime investigation agencies

Click any tile to open the agency's LinkedIn page (or official website where no LinkedIn exists).

Structural note: Serbia is an EU candidate country, not a member — so its Europol and Eurojust relationships run through cooperation/operational agreements rather than full membership, and it sits outside the Schengen Information System. Domestically, the Ministry of the Interior (MUP) runs the Criminal Police Directorate (UKP), inside which sits the Service for Combating Organised Crime (SBPOK) — Serbia's nearest equivalent to a dedicated OC bureau. BIA (the security-intelligence agency) is unusual among the countries in this series: it holds explicit police-style powers to gather evidence for criminal proceedings, not just intelligence collection.

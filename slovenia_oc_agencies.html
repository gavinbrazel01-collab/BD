Slovenia — organised crime investigation agencies

Click any tile to open the agency's official website. Slovenia is a small but strategically critical country at the crossroads of the Balkan route — a primary transit corridor for drugs, human trafficking and arms moving from the Western Balkans into Western Europe. The Criminal Police Directorate (CPD) and its National Bureau of Investigation (NPU) lead all serious OC investigations nationally.

web ↗ CPD — HT Unit Human Trafficking Investigation Unit (within CPD) Investigates all HT cases under Article 113 KZ-1 (trafficking) and Article 175 (pimping / exploitation). 43 potential trafficking investigations in 2024 (up from 36 in 2023). Primary victims: Ukrainian, Romanian, Hungarian, Balkan and Dominican Republic nationals. Works with MNZ Anti-Trafficking Service Office and government-funded NGOs

web ↗ CPD — Drug Unit Drug Crime Investigation Unit (within CPD) Investigates drug trafficking networks — cocaine via Port of Koper (Balkan Cartel), cannabis, heroin and synthetic drugs. Balkan Cartel dominates cocaine and cannabis supply in Slovenia. Raids in 2023–2024 confirmed cannabis cultivation networks. Port of Koper is a key EU cocaine entry point

Russia and China-linked cybercriminal groups have targeted Slovenian government agencies and financial entities. Supports NPU on financial crime digital evidence

Prosecution authorities Tožilstvo
web ↗ SDT Specializirano državno tožilstvo — Specialised State Prosecutor's Office Slovenia's specialist prosecution body for the most complex crimes — OC, serious economic crime, corruption and HT. Requires special organisation and highest-level prosecutorial efficiency. Directs NPU investigations. Initiated criminal proceedings against 11 suspected traffickers and 2 legal entities in 2024. Primary contact for Eurojust JITs

Analysis tools in use across agencies

Analytical tools (NPU / CPD) NPU and CPD use criminal intelligence analytical software for OC network mapping and financial crime tracing. Specific commercial platforms are not publicly disclosed — tools are understood to include Europol-standard analytical systems used across EU member states
SIS II / Schengen / SIENA Full Schengen Information System access. SIENA (Europol's secure information exchange network) is used by CPD and NPU for Europol operational intelligence sharing. IPCD manages all SIS and Europol information flows nationally
Financial intelligence (UPPD / 2024 EU project) In 2024 the government joined an EU-funded Financial Intelligence against Human Trafficking project — using financial analysis to optimise HT investigations. UPPD and CPD cooperate on financial tracing for HT and OC asset recovery under the Criminal Assets Act

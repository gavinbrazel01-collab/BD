Sweden — organised crime investigation agencies

Click any tile to open the agency's LinkedIn page (or official website where no LinkedIn exists).

Structural note: Sweden has operated a single, nationalised Police Authority (Polismyndigheten) since 2015, organised into 7 geographic police regions plus national departments. The National Operations Department (NOA) is the central hub directing and coordinating the fight against organised crime nationally and internationally — there is no separate federal/state split. Säpo (Security Service) split off as its own agency in the same 2015 reform. Sweden's gang/OC shooting and bombing crisis has driven major resourcing increases to NOA and regional Serious Crime Units in recent years.

Analysis tools in use across agencies

i2 Analyst's Notebook Used by NOA and regional Serious Crime Units — link analysis, timelines, and entity charting for gang-network and OC investigations, including the "cylinder model" used to identify drivers of criminal influence in flagged areas
RAR / SIS II link RAR (Rationell anmälningsrutin) is Sweden's police case-management system; connected to the EU Schengen Information System for cross-border lookups with Denmark, Norway, Finland and the wider Schengen area
Financial data fusion (EBM / Tullverket) Ekobrottsmyndigheten and Tullverket combine bank, tax and customs data with NOA intelligence for money-laundering and asset-tracing cases tied to gang and OC networks

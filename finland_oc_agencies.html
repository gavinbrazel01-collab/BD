Finland — organised crime investigation agencies

Click any tile to open the agency's LinkedIn page (or official website where no LinkedIn exists).

Structural note: Finland's police is a single national agency under the Ministry of the Interior, run by the National Police Board (Poliisihallitus) with 11 local police departments plus 2 national units — the National Bureau of Investigation (KRP/NBI) and the Police University College. SUPO (the security intelligence service) became a fully independent national police unit reporting directly to the Ministry of the Interior in 2016, separate from Poliisihallitus.

National investigation & intelligence agencies valtakunnalliset yksiköt
in ↗ KRP / NBI Keskusrikospoliisi (National Bureau of Investigation) Finland's principal organised-crime, financial-intelligence and serious-crime agency, headquartered in Vantaa with field offices in Tampere, Turku, Mariehamn, Joensuu, Oulu & Rovaniemi. Criminal Investigation Division covers OC, homicide and financial crime; Criminal Intelligence Division houses the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) and international affairs section. Runs Finland's only forensic laboratory and the national group specialising in human trafficking.
